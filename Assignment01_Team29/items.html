CPU

A CPU (Central Processing Unit) is the core component of a computer, responsible for executing instructions and performing calculations. It processes data by fetching, decoding, executing, and storing instructions, enabling software to run and perform tasks.

Raspberry Pi

A Raspberry Pi is a low-cost, compact computer ideal for DIY projects and learning. It's versatile, running a Linux-based OS, and features various I/O options. It's popular for coding, robotics, and home automation.

Hard Disk Drive

A hard drive, often abbreviated as HDD, is a data storage device found in computers and other electronic devices. It uses spinning magnetic disks to store and retrieve digital information. Hard drives offer high-capacity storage for files, applications, and operating systems, making them a key component in most computers.

Casette

The cassette, commonly known as the cassette tape, is an analog magnetic tape recording format introduced in the 1960s. Popular for music distribution and playback in the 70s and 80s, it became synonymous with portable music thanks to devices like the Walkman. Its compact design and affordability made it a favorite for personal audio consumption before the rise of CDs.

PSP 1000

The PSP 1000, also known as the PlayStation Portable, is Sony's debut handheld gaming console launched in 2004. With its impressive graphics and multimedia capabilities, it became popular for both gaming and media consumption. It features a large screen, Wi-Fi connectivity, and supports UMD discs for game and media playback.

Crypto Wallet

A crypto wallet is a digital tool that allows users to store, manage, and transact with cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum. It keeps private and public keys, facilitating transactions and tracking ownership. Wallets can be hardware-based for added security or software-based, available as desktop, mobile, or web applications.
